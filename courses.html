NEET
NEET is the acronym for The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test is the new entrance examination at National level to qualify for medical courses. The Ministry of Human Resource Development in India, had introduced NEET as the eligibility test for the following Undergraduate Medical Courses in different medical colleges all over India
  • M.B.B.S : Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery
  • B.D.S. : Bachelor of Dental Surgery
  • B.A.M.S : Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine & Surgery
  • B.H.M.S : Bachelor of Homeopathic Medicine & Surgery
  • B.U.M.S : Bachelor of Unani Medicine & Surgery
  • B.N.Y.S : Bachelor of Naturopathy & Yoga Science
  • B.S.M.S : Bachelor of Siddha Medicine & Surgery
However, this year (2021) , NEET will also include B.Sc Nursing and B.Sc Life Sciences.
NTA also conducts NEET PG for admission into Post Graduate Medical Courses like M.S. & M.D in Private and Government Medical colleges of India.
The National testing Agency (NTA) regulated the NEET exam and will prepare the question paper based on the syllabus prescribed by Medical Council of India (MCI)
Why NEET?

NEET has been created to replace AIPMT and other Under Graduate level Medical Entrance Examinations with a vision to create a single entrance exam for all students who are medical aspirants in India. Different medical examinations posed challenges both for the parents and the aspirants with a different syllabus, different exam centres and different style of questions papers. NEET addresses to solve all such issues and avoid confusions. This is one score that will be taken into consideration by the medical colleges the aspirants wish to apply for.

NEET Exam Pattern

NTA will announce NEET exam pattern 2021 in the information brochure which is to be released anytime soon.

As per NEET 2020 Exam pattern, candidates were given OMR sheet to mark their responses and the Exam was based in Pen and Paper mode on core subjects studied in Class 11th and 12th. Candidates are not allowed to bring their own stationery at the NEET centre and the same was provided inside the examination hall only .

Total 180 questions are assigned to Physics, Chemistry & Biology (Botany and Zoology) with 4 marks for every correct Answer, 1 mark was deducted for every wrong Answer and Nil Mark for un-attempted questions

NEET Eligibility Criteria
  • As per NEET Eligibility Criteria, a candidate must have passed class 12 in PCB with a minimum of 50% marks. Also, the candidates must have completed 17 years as on December 31, 2021 but the upper age limit criteria have been removed by NTA which enables candidates above 25 years of age to fill NEET 2021 Application Form.
  • Other than Indian Nationals, NRIs, OCIs, PIOs & Foreign Nationals are also eligible to apply NEET 2021. There is no limit on no. of attempts and if a candidate is eligible then he/ she can appear for the examination as many times.
Qualification & Marks
  • Students who have passed the class XII qualifying exam are eligible to apply for NEET 2021. Candidates must have Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology as their subjects in Class XII and should have passed in all the subjects. Class XII is the basic qualifying exam, but the qualifying marks differ according to the candidates’ category.
  • Candidates need to fill the appropriate NEET Qualifying Examination Codes in the online application form. NIOS candidates and those who opted for Biology as an additional subject are now also eligible for NEET 2021

JEE Main Exam Pattern 2021

NTA has released the new JEE Main 2021 exam pattern for B.Tech, B. Arch and B. Planning papers.

The exam pattern of JEE Main 2021 is divided into JEE Paper 1 pattern for B.Tech and JEE Paper 2 pattern for B. Arch and B. Planning.

As per JEE Main exam pattern 2021, each subject will have 20 MCQs and 10 Numerical Value Questions and out of 10 only 5 have to be done. According to the JEE Main 2021 exam pattern, both the papers will be conducted in online mode except for the Drawing test for B.Arch.

JEE Main 2021 will be conducted in 4 sessions

February March April May

Paper 1 for B.E/B.Tech. and Paper 2 for B.Arch or B.Plan

JEE Main Exam Pattern 2021- What's New?
  • As per the latest JEE Main 2021 exam pattern, paper 1 will have a total of 90 questions, 25 each from Physics, Chemistry, and Maths. Candidates have to attempt only 75 questions.
  • Each subject will have 20 MCQs and 10 numerical questions. 5 out of 10 numerical questions are optional.
  • Numerical questions don't have negative marking.
  • JEE Main 2021 for paper 1 (B.Tech) will be conducted for 300 marks, while paper 2 (B.Arch/B.Plan) will be conducted separately for 400 marks each.
  • Now, JEE Main 2021 will be held in 13 languages
